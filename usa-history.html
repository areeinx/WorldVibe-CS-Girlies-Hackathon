American History

From colonial times through revolution to modern superpower.

A Nation's Journey

The United States has a relatively short but dramatic history, from colonial settlements to becoming a world superpower. Each era has left its mark on American identity and values.

Key Historical Periods

  • Colonial Era: European settlement and establishment of thirteen colonies.
  • American Revolution: 1776 independence from British rule.
  • Expansion Era: Westward expansion and manifest destiny.
  • Civil War: 1861-1865, conflict that ended slavery.
  • Industrial Age: Rise as an industrial and economic powerhouse.
  • Modern Era: World Wars, Cold War, and emergence as superpower.

Historic Sites

Independence Hall in Philadelphia, the Lincoln Memorial, Civil War battlefields, and Ellis Island represent pivotal moments in American history that shaped the nation.
