India — Overview

A vast country with diverse landscapes and cultures.

Land of Diversity

India is the world's second-most populous country and the seventh-largest by area. It stretches from the Himalayan mountains to tropical beaches, encompassing countless religions, languages, and cultures.

Geographic Regions

  • The Himalayas: Snow-capped mountains with trekking and spiritual sites.
  • Northern Plains: Home to Delhi, the Ganges, and Taj Mahal.
  • Southern Regions: Tropical, with backwaters, plantations, and temples.
  • Western Coast: Arabian Sea beaches and cosmopolitan cities like Mumbai.
  • Eastern Regions: Cultural heritage, tea plantations, and Bengal.

Cultural Significance

India is the birthplace of Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism. It has influenced world philosophies, mathematics, astronomy, and the arts for thousands of years.
