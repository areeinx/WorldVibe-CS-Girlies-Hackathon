Japan — Overview

From Hokkaido to Okinawa, explore Japan's islands and regions.

Island Nation

Japan is an island nation in East Asia consisting of four main islands: Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, and Shikoku, plus thousands of smaller islands. Its unique geography and isolation shaped a distinct culture.

Major Regions

  • Hokkaido: Japan's northernmost island, famous for skiing and nature.
  • Honshu: The largest island, home to Tokyo, Kyoto, and Mount Fuji.
  • Kyoto: Japan's cultural heart with thousands of temples and traditional districts.
  • Osaka & Kobe: Modern cities on Honshu's western coast.
  • Okinawa: Southern tropical island with beaches and unique culture.

Geography

Japan sits on the Pacific Ring of Fire with frequent earthquakes. The country features mountains, forests, and a long coastline. Cherry blossoms and seasonal changes are integral to Japanese aesthetics.
